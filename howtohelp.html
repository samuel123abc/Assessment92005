1. Join or Support Conservation Groups

Organisations across New Zealand are working to save native species. You can donate, volunteer, or spread the word.

  • Predator Free NZ
  • Forest & Bird
  • Department of Conservation (DOC)

2. Trap Predators at Home

Even in cities, rats and possums threaten native birds. You can install traps in your backyard and join your local predator-free group.

Visit Trap.nz

3. Plant Native Trees

Native plants provide food and shelter for birds, lizards, and insects. Replace exotic species with harakeke, mānuka, kōwhai and others.

4. Keep Cats Indoors

Domestic cats hunt birds, skinks, and insects. Keep your cat inside at night, add a bell to its collar, or build a catio (cat patio).

5. Reduce Waste and Pollution

Plastic and chemical waste harms marine and freshwater species. Choose reusable items, reduce pesticide use, and keep waterways clean.
