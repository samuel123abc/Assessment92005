Kiwi (Apteryx spp.)

Status: Nationally Vulnerable

The kiwi is New Zealand’s iconic flightless bird. All five species are threatened due to predation by stoats, habitat loss, and slow breeding.

Kākāpō (Strigops habroptilus)

Status: Critically Endangered

The kākāpō is a large, nocturnal, flightless parrot. Only about 250 individuals remain, all closely monitored on predator-free islands.

Māui Dolphin (Cephalorhynchus hectori maui)

Status: Critically Endangered

One of the rarest dolphins in the world, with fewer than 60 individuals. Threatened by fishing nets and pollution.

Takahē (Porphyrio hochstetteri)

Status: Nationally Vulnerable

Once thought extinct, the takahē is a rare flightless bird found in Fiordland and managed sanctuaries. Intensive recovery has brought the population to over 400.

Tuatara (Sphenodon punctatus)

Status: At Risk - Relict

Not a lizard, but a unique reptile from the dinosaur era. Survives only on predator-free offshore islands and special sanctuaries.
